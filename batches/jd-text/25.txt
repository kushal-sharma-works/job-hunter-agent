Mobile SDK Lead | Android | Kotlin
Amsterdam | €100,000 - €115,000
Question.
At what point did you become more interested in the SDK than the app?
More interested in:
Architecture
Modularity
Performance
Tooling
Developer Experience
better than building another screen? Because that's exactly the point this role is aimed at.
I'm working with a cybersecurity company whose product is a mobile SDK embedded inside banking applications.
Not a mobile app team.
Not a feature factory.
The SDK is the product.
It runs inside some of the most sensitive mobile environments in the world, helping financial institutions understand whether a device can be trusted, whether malware is present, and whether suspicious activity is taking place.
This creates a very different set of engineering challenge.
You don't control the app.
You don't control the device.
You don't control the release cycle.
Yet the SDK still needs to be stable, performant, backwards compatible and developer-friendly.
That's where you come in.
I'm looking for a technically strong Android engineer who has led people before, Not an architect, not a manager.
A genuine Android engineer who can provide technical direction, mentor others, make good decisions and still enjoy getting hands-on with Kotlin.
You'll probably have spent time around:
SDKs
Shared mobile platforms
Libraries
Large Android codebases
Architecture & modularisation
Developer tooling
The kind of engineer who asks:
"How do we solve this once, properly?"
rather than:
"How quickly can we ship it?"
Small team of 4, to be 6. Serious Android engineering. Interesting problems.
Sound like you? Happy to tell you a bit more.
